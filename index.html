Modular Synth Work:

Moog Mother 32 performance

Description: I wanted to make a rather chiptuny/retro styled music for this performance. Basically like a song you'd here in an "Alien Invasion" styled game. For this, I used a synthesizer called the Moog Mother 32. What I did in order to make this performance a thing was first experiment with the equipment by seeing what kinds of settings, patterns, musical notes, and overall tweakings. This was so I could see how I am able to create the type of sound that fits with the kind of feel/atmosphere I wanted for this moment. With the knowledge of the types of sounds I can and want to create for my performance in my mind, I decide on what kind of structure I want the music to be in, as well as in what order I'd like to synthesize said sounds. I also make it so I remember the kind of settings/tweaking I would want in order to synthesize each specific type of sound. After that, I get ready to perform and record, and I do this multiple times until I get the best performance/result. This is that best result.

Trumpet and Modular

Description: The way that I was able to make this performance possible is first planning what kind of overall feel and atmosphere I wanted it to be the music in. I wanted the overall feel and atmosphere to be like that of being in the depths of an ocean. Mysterious, unnerving, but also soothing at the same time. Then, I would plan out what type of sounds I would like to create in order to produce this kind of feel/atmosphere. I would then layout the necessary modular synthesizers, or any recording equipment in general necessary for these sounds to be produced. I essentially played around with the equipment for a while until I was satisfied with the kind of sounds I was producing. I then layout a plan on how I would adjust the settings of said equipment in order to have the performance actually work out. I perform multiple times and record myself doing it until I get the best result, which is this right here.
